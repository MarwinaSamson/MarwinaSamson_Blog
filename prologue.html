"The Night Before"

The evening before it all begins

My bag is packed. I check it a third time — not because I think I forgot something, but because I do not know what else to do with my hands. Tomorrow we leave early. Tomorrow is the first day of the thing we have been talking about for months, and now that it is here, I am not sure what I was expecting.

I sit on the edge of my bed and look at the bag by the door. It looks too small for seven days. Or maybe I am overthinking. I probably am.

There is a specific kind of quiet that comes the night before something big. Not peaceful — just waiting.

Continued

My classmates and I have been together long enough that I know how each of them handles nerves. Some of them are probably still awake right now, too. Someone is definitely checking their outfit one more time. Someone else has already fallen asleep out of sheer stubbornness. I do not know which one I envy more.

I open my notes app and start typing — not anything in particular, just words. What do I want to get out of this? What am I afraid of? The answers come faster than I expect. I want to understand what it actually feels like to stand inside the world I am studying for. And I am afraid that it will feel nothing like I imagined.

Both of those things feel true at the same time. That seems like a good place to start.

I set my alarm. I close my eyes. The room is the same room it always is, but tomorrow it will be the last room I wake up in before everything begins.

I do not sleep for a long time.
