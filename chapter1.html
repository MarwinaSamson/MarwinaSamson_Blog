3:00 AM · Leaving the house

Arrival · NAIA I

Meeting DJM & the Dorm

NAIA · Afternoon

We meet the DJM representative who will guide our tour. They are older, precise, and carry the kind of calm that makes itineraries happen. They lead us to the dorm where we'll stay for the week.

The dorm smells faintly of detergent and travel. Bunk beds, shared sinks, new roommates introductions — the kind of small rituals that feel like the start of something.

— 3 —
Accommodations

We unpack one small bag each. A map on the wall shows the city like a promise. Someone discovers a stray charger and becomes a hero.

Luneta Park

Sightseeing · Free Day

Luneta smells of grass and roasted peanuts. We walk under a blue sky and the monument looks both smaller and larger than I expected. There are vendors and old men feeding pigeons and the city feels kindly.

Day 0 · Afternoon I

Intramuros

Walled City · Walking

Intramuros feels like a pocket of another century. Old stone, narrow streets, horses, and tourists with cameras. We tramp along and learn the contours of history in footsteps.

— 7 —
Explore

We enter a small museum and someone points at an old photograph, saying 'my grandmother had that dress'. It feels like the city folds into our family stories for a moment.

Day 0 · Late Afternoon I

Manila Cathedral

Quiet · Small Crowds

The cathedral is cool and smells faintly of wax. We walk in quietly, shoeless in some corners, and I feel the hush of people thinking their private prayers.

— 9 —
Stillness

A bell rings somewhere and we all look up. For a while we are very small and grateful to be small.

Day 0 · Evening I

Mall of Asia

Sea breeze · Neon

MOA is loud and bright. We eat something greasy and walk by the bay. There is ferris wheel light and a salty wind and the city feels larger than it did that morning.

— 11 —
Evening

We buy souvenirs. Someone takes a picture of the sunset and it looks like a postcard. I keep thinking about how much can fit into one day.

Night · Return I

Back to the Dorm

Night · Rest

We come back tired and full. People share earbuds and stories. For a moment in the dark corridor I think this is the first time of many first times.
